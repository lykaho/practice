Malcolm X

May 19, 1925 – February 21, 1965

Human-Rights activist

Malcolm Little, better known as Malcolm X, was an African American Muslim minister, and human rights activist who was a popular figure during the civil rights movement. He is best known for his time spent as a vocal spokesman for the Nation of Islam.

Timeline

May 19 1925 Malcolm Little was born on May 19, in Omaha, Nebraska, to Earl and Louise Little (née Louisa Norton).
September 28 1931 Earl Little (Malcom X's father) fatally struck by "streetcar" - Malcolm later says that the Ku Klux Klan was behind it.
1939 Malcolm Little was one of the best students in his junior high school, but he dropped out of 8th grade after a white teacher told him that his aspirations of being a lawyer were no realistic goal for a nigger.
1946 Malcolm Little is arrested for burglary
1948 Malcolm's siblings, introduce him to the words of the Nation of Islam's leader, Elijah Muhammad, who is himself in prison
August 7 1952 Malcolm X is paroled and released from prison
1952 Malcolm Little changes surname to "X" - after joining the Nation of Islam. He starts recruiting for the organization
1953 Having tripled the membership of the Detroit temple in under a year, Malcolm is appointed assistant minister there.
1955 Malcolm X attends the first Conference of the Non-aligned Nations in Bandung, Indonesia.
1957 A Member is savagely beaten by police. Malcolm joins a contingent of Muslims at the 28th Precinct headquarters in Harlem, where he demands that Hinton receive medical attention. Hinton is eventually taken by ambulance to a nearby hospital. Satisfied that his demands have been met, Malcolm disperses the crowd with a wave of his hand.
1958 Malcolm X marries Betty Sanders. Their first child is being born.
1959 Malcolm makes trips abroad, visiting Ghana, Sudan, Nigeria, Iran, Syria, Egypt, and the United Arab Republic; illness prevents him from traveling to Mecca. Meets with President Gamal Abdel Nasser of Egypt.
1961 Malcolm becomes National Representative of the Nation of Islam.
1962 Malcolm confirms that Elijah Muhammad has engaged in repeated adultery and had children with at least three of his secretaries. "I felt almost out of my mind," Malcolm said.
1963 The events around Elijah Muhammad cause several conflicts between the two.
January - March 1964 Marlcom is being removed as the National Representative of the Nation of Islam and being suspended indefinitely later.
April 1964 Malcolm delivers his famous election year "Ballot or the Bullet" speech, then leaves for a five-week tour. During that he also visits Mecca. He changes his name to El-Hajj Malik El-Shabazz. After visiting he expresses a different attitude about race.
Summer - Fall 1964 His life is threatened by the Nation of Islam. He forms the Organization of Afro-American Unity (OAAU) and spends nearly five months in Africa.
January 1965 Malcolm flies to Los Angeles to meet with Gladys Towles Roots and two Nation of Islam secretaries who are filing paternity suits against Elijah Muhammad.
Feb 21 1965 Malcolm X is assassinated while speaking at an OAAU rally in Harlem; three members of the Nation of Islam are later convicted.
